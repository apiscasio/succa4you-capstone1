What Are Succulents?

Though most of us refer to them by the common term, “desert plants,” the term “succulents” has grown in popularity. In horticulture, succulents are known as water storage plants. These plants store water in their thick, fleshy leaves. But that may be all that succulents have in common, seeing as how they come in a seemingly endless array of shapes, sizes, and colors.

But Why Choose Succulents instead of regular "Pretty Flowers"?

Firstly,

Succulents can survive your BLACK thumb (if you do have). Because they are low maintenance.

Secondly,

They are sculptural and graphic, which makes them work so well in mid-century modern and industrial-style settings. It's no surprise we associate them with this type of architecture since West Coast modern houses boast these indigenous, desert-like landscapes.

Thirdly,

They look cool in concrete planters. And we love concrete! Especially how it lends itself to clean geometry.

Lastly,

They look like they are from an alien planet and that is just plain cool. Nature never disappoints.
